Bengali Chicken Curry with Potatoes

Description

Imagine a delightful evening where the aroma of spices fills the air, as you savor a hearty bowl of Bengali Chicken Curry with potatoes. This dish begins with a symphony of flavors as onions are gently sautéed in rich, golden olive oil until they turn perfectly translucent. The addition of a fragrant ginger-garlic paste intensifies the depth of the dish, creating a robust base that promises a culinary journey like no other.

Serve this Bengali Chicken Curry hot, and enjoy the symphony of flavors that dance on your palate with every mouthful. It's a comforting, soul-warming experience, perfect for any occasion.

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 large onions, diced
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 large tomatoes, diced
  • 1 teaspoon cayenne pepper, or more to taste
  • 1 teaspoon curry powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon ground turmeric
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 4 skinless, boneless chicken breast halves - cut into bite-size pieces
  • 2 large red-skinned potatoes, chopped
  • ½ cup fresh cilantro

Steps

  1. Heat the olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat.
  2. Cook and stir the onions in the hot oil until translucent, about 5 minutes.
  3. Add the ginger-garlic paste and continue cooking for another 5 minutes.
  4. Reduce heat to medium.
  5. Stir the tomatoes into the mixture and cook until the tomatoes are pulpy, 5 to 10 minutes.
  6. Season with the cayenne pepper, curry powder, garam masala, turmeric, and cumin.
  7. Cook and stir for another 5 minutes.
  8. Add the chicken and potatoes to the mixture in the skillet.
  9. Simmer, stirring occasionally, until the potatoes are tender and the chicken is no longer pink in the center, about 20 minutes.
  10. Sprinkle the cilantro over the mixture.
  11. Continue simmering for another 10 minutes.
  12. Serve hot.
